The office lunch playbook for Jubilee Town & Izmir Town

7 min read · by Kitchen by Iqra, a home kitchen in Jubilee Town

Quick answer: the calmest way to feed an office in Jubilee Town, Izmir Town or Tricon Village is to decide the whole week's menu on Monday morning, fix one delivery slot, and budget PKR 350–500 per head per day for homemade food. Our free weekly planner does exactly this in about two minutes.

The 11:45 panic, and why it happens

Every office around Canal Road knows the scene: it's 11:45, nobody ordered, the group chat is voting between three fast-food apps, and whoever's turn it is to decide has stopped answering. The food arrives at 1:40, half the order is wrong, and by 3 PM the whole floor is in an oil coma. The problem isn't the team — it's deciding daily what should be decided weekly.

Step 1: budget honestly, per head per day

With homemade plates, the math is friendly. On our menu, a daal or sabzi day costs PKR 300–400 a head, a biryani day PKR 400, and a karahi or handi day PKR 500–600. Add roti, salad or raita at PKR 60 each where the dish needs it. A balanced week averages out around PKR 400–450 per person per day — for ten people, roughly PKR 20,000–22,500 a week, with delivery free at that volume in our service areas. Compare that with fast food at PKR 700–900 a head and the spreadsheet argues for daal all by itself.

Step 2: rotate like a household, not a food court

A week that nobody complains about usually looks like a home week: start Monday light (daal mash with fresh roti), give Tuesday some occasion (chicken biryani with raita), keep Wednesday gentle (karhi pakora or a seasonal sabzi), let Thursday be the rich day (white handi or karahi), and end Friday generous (qorma with salad, or pulao with shami kababs). Two meat days, two light days, one festive day. People stop dreading lunch and start checking the calendar.

Decide once, eat all week. The planner is just a dastarkhwan with checkboxes.

Step 3: fix one delivery slot and protect it

Food for ten arriving in three batches means three interruptions and cold rice for somebody. Pick a single slot — most offices around here choose 1:00–1:30 PM — and have everything arrive together. Because we cook each morning's batch after orders close at 11 AM, one slot also means your food goes straight from handi to bike to table, no waiting under a heat lamp.

Step 4: keep the plan changeable

Plans meet reality: half the team is at a client visit Thursday, or Tuesday suddenly needs to impress a guest. A good weekly system bends without breaking. In our planner, any day's dish or plate count can be edited until 11 AM on that day — change it online and resend, or just message Iqra on WhatsApp. The plan is a rhythm, not a contract.

Step 5: count the extras per head

The quiet cause of lunch disputes is never the salan — it's the last roti. Order sides per person (two rotis a head with daal, one raita per biryani plate, salad on rich days) so nothing runs out. We add these per-head automatically for office plans when you mention team size in the order note.

Frequently asked questions

What does office lunch cost per head in 2026?
With homemade food: PKR 350–500 per head per day depending on the rotation — daal and sabzi days at the low end, karahi and handi days at the top. Sides are PKR 60 each.
Is delivery free for offices?
Yes — on 10+ plates per day within Jubilee Town, Bahria Town, Izmir Town, Tricon Village, Sherawala Heights and Iqbal Avenue. Below that, delivery is charged by location.
Can we change the menu mid-week?
Any day can be changed until 11 AM that day — edit the planner and resend, or message us on WhatsApp.
